# Open Governance Questions

These questions should remain visible until the long-term operator is selected.

## Ownership

- Who owns the WorkJax name?
- Who owns the code?
- Who owns the data compiled by the platform?
- Who owns or controls the production domain?
- Who controls the GitHub and Vercel organizations?
- Who is authorized to approve architectural changes?

## Operations

- Which ecosystem partner will operate the platform?
- Who is responsible for daily sync failures?
- Who reviews low-confidence or conflicting records?
- Who responds to incorrect opportunity or event information?
- What is the content freshness standard?
- What budget supports hosting, database, APIs, legal review, and moderation?
- What service-level expectations will be published?

## Profiles and Safety

- Will Connect Jax launch with adults only?
- Will minor profiles be excluded, private, or restricted to a verified network?
- What guardian consent process is acceptable?
- Which external contact links are allowed?
- Who moderates profiles and reports?
- How quickly must urgent reports be reviewed?
- What information may appear publicly?
- What verification is required for employer and school affiliations?

## Partnerships

- Which organizations could provide structured opportunity data?
- Which employers use applicant tracking systems with public job feeds?
- Which event calendars provide RSS, API, or structured data?
- Is there a partner willing to provide a periodic approved export?
- Can regional organizations agree on shared data standards?

## Success and Evaluation

- What is the geographic boundary?
- Which users are prioritized during the pilot?
- What constitutes a successful referral?
- Will WorkJax measure application clicks or confirmed placements?
- How will employers provide outcome data without creating duplicate work?
